Even now, watching her crouch in the shadow of these behemoths, sifting the sand with her fingers like that is where the answers lay, I'm not sure if she's really here.

Or even if I'm really here.

All this time spent together is wreaking havoc on me. I forget what has happened and what hasn't. I tell her about conversations we've never had, or maybe we have. Maybe I just dreamt them.

I can't remember if we've kissed or not, and at this point, I'm too afraid to ask.

Then I feel something in the sand, hard and inorganic - a metal box buried just beneath one of the dishes. I open it, and find it full of letters, papers, all from the old man...

And I am briefly sure this is all real.
