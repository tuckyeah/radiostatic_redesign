After I pulled the trigger, I collapsed on top of her body and there I wept for forty days and forty nights until it felt like the cells of my flesh were mingling with hers, my tears and her Vitreous Humours dancing now upon our cheeks. I remember looking into her Milky Eyes and seeing the Fear still in them, laid bare like a raw nerve.

Jeremiah came to me in a dream shortly thereafter and he told me what to do.

I buried my beloved in the reams of our research, reading and savoring each page we had written before gently adding it to the growing pile in the middle of the house.



After allowing myself a moment to marvel at the sheer size and breadth of our work,

I doused it in gasoline...

and watched it burn.
