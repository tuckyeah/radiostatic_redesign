Trip Report 4: Devil's Weed (Datura stramonium)

Dosage: two roots, cut from plant outside camper


Exercised extraordinary caution with this one. I have been feeling fragile of late, and the visions promised by this plant are vivid and unforgiving. I must confess to a degree of nervousness, so I carried some of our journals with me into the desert and traced your handwriting with my finger, feeling you pulsing through the ink.



Without warning, I was assaulted with the plant's power.

The desert beneath my feet began to undulate, like it too was breathing. I climbed onto one of the satellite dishes in hopes that I could hide there, but when I touched it, I felt it too was warm and soft. I could feel a heartbeat in its metal legs.

I felt the hot breath of the Wolves upon my neck and the backs of my knees. They were circling closer, closer, just like they were That Night.

My head was throbbing and my vision blurring and I looked down to see the shotgun resting against my shoulder like an extension of myself and then I looked up to see you in front of me.

I screamed and closed my eyes, and when I opened them again, the shotgun had transformed into our Sweet Jeremiah, a squalling babe in my arms.
