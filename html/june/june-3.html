We set up camp a ways from the trailer/bar, but I could still see its glow on the horizon like a setting sun. Nearby, a generator hummed but otherwise the night was silent.

I slipped off my shoes and buried my feet in the sand. It was still warm from the day.

"So you really think this is the place?" I asked.

"It has to be. You heard what Leopold said about looking for his son. This is what we're meant to be doing."

"Charlie, I'm not entirely sure that old man has a son at all. Probably just a collection of animal bones in the shape of one in his bedroom."

He turned to me and gave me an affectionate smile. "Let's just wait it out, check out those satellites tomorrow. Can't you feel the energy here? We're onto something, June." He dug his hands into the sand. "The ground is actually buzzing."

"Charlie. That's the generator powering the trailer."


When he laughs, I can't help but smile myself.

"Hey June?"

"Yes, Charlie?"

"Do you remember how we met?"

"What kind of question is that?"

"I was just asking. Do you ever wonder if sometimes maybe you're asleep and you don't know it?"

"That's an even stranger question."

"You're right. Nevermind."

"...Hey June?"

"Yes, Charlie?"

"I'm glad we met. Dream or no dream."

"...Me too, Charlie. Me too."
