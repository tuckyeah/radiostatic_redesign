Charlie showed me the 'reports' and we read and reread the pages until we were sure we had panned every last flake of gold from them.

"Charlie... This man almost certainly killed his son himself. I mean, look at this. This is insane. This can't be the boywho we're looking for."

Charlie didn't say anything, and that in and of itself was disconcerting. He just kept flipping through the pages. At first, I thought he hadn't heard me, but then he slowly nodded his head.

"You're right."

This wasn't like Charlie. He never gave up so easily. Something was wrong.

"So then what are we doing here?"

"What are you talking about? We're searching for a missing person, Charlie--"

"For who?" He demanded with such a violence in his voice it almost frightened me.

Charlie reached into his shirt pocket and dug out the worn piece of paper we had been carrying with us for how many miles, the missing persons poster with the black and white, Xerox bleached face.

"You can't even make out the face anymore, it's just shapes... like a Rorschach drawing."

He was right, of course: there was nothing left of the face but the black smudges that hinted at the edges of features, a portrait in negative space.

"Who are we looking for, June? Can you remember?"

I studied it long and hard and came to the alarming realization that I could not remember the face either.

How many times had I looked at this very poster? How many days had I traveled with it in my breast pocket, checking it just before I went to bed, showing it to potential leads and witnesses and now...

There was just an empty space where the memory should have been.
