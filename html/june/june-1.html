June
Variable

Charlie and I met in his dreams. He was asleep, and I... well, I don't really know. I suppose I didn't exist, at least not really, as strange as that feels to say.

I have vague memories of feeling lost, adrift in a boundless sea, feeling I was missing something without ever quite knowing what that something was.

When we crossed paths, I realized had finally found an anchor, a compass, a magnetic North with which to orient myself.

There is a something in him that I cannot seem to look away from. Being near him is like the white hot heat of the sun, and I fear I may have become dependent on it - and, by extension, on him - for my survial.

Don't get me wrong. It's not as though I was some sort of damsel in distress, waiting for something like love to find me and take me away from this life.

I wish there were a way to put this that sounded somewhat less helpless, but I have found it best to be simple and direct.

It is the nature of dreams to be difficult to pin down, and as such, I'm afraid neither Charlie nor I can recall much about the details surrounding our initial meeting. We only know that we did, and we have been partners ever since.

If there is one thing I have learned in working with Charlie, it is not to put too much weight in feelings, or on the present moment.

A life is made out of a series of moments, and by putting too much weight on one over any other can dangerously tip the center of balance and alter our entire life's course.

That all being said, we have been on a quest for as long as we can remember (which may or may not be very long at all). As it's Charlie's dream, it's Charlie who is driving our search, but I am a more than willing companion.

The details are hazy, but Charlie is insistent that we will know what we are searching for when we find it.

I cannot say why I trust him, or even if I believe him, but I am nevertheless drawn to him the way the moon is to the sun.

Besides, it's not as if I have much else to do.
